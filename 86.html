  • Let p: The switch S1 is closed.
  • Let q: The switch S2 is closed.
  • Let r: The switch S3 is closed.

ONLY FOR Explanation: The first step in any logic circuit problem is to translate the physical components into logical statements. We assign standard letters like p, q, and r to represent the "closed" state of each switch. A closed switch means current can flow, which is equivalent to a logical True or 1.

Step 2: Constructing the Symbolic Form. [1/2 Mark]

From the given circuit diagram:

  • Switches S2 and S3 are connected in series. This is represented by (q ∧ r).
  • Switch S1 is connected in parallel with the series combination of S2 and S3.

Therefore, the symbolic form of the given switching circuit is:

p ∨ (q ∧ r)

ONLY FOR Explanation: Analyze the circuit layout carefully. Switches placed one after the other in the same wire branch are in "series", which corresponds to logical AND (∧). Branches that offer alternative paths for the current are in "parallel", which corresponds to logical OR (∨).

Professor Wadhwa's Note:

Common Mistake Alert! My dear students, a very common error made in board exams is using 'T' and 'F' in an input-output table. Remember, for switching circuits, examiners specifically look for 1 and 0. Using T/F here might cost you precious marks!

Board Exam Twist: Sometimes the examiner might ask you to "interpret" the result. In that case, look at the final column. If it's all 1s, the lamp is always on irrespective of switch positions (Tautology). If it's all 0s, the lamp will never glow (Contradiction). Stay sharp, think logically, and you will score full marks!
