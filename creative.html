Question 1 (i)

Solution:

From the given circuit diagram, we have two resistors connected in series: R₁ = 4Ω and R₂ = 6Ω, connected to a 10V battery.

Step 1: Calculate the total resistance

For resistors in series, the total resistance Rtotal = R₁ + R₂

Rtotal = 4Ω + 6Ω = 10Ω

Step 2: Calculate the total current

Using Ohm's Law: V = I × R, therefore I = V / R

I = 10V / 10Ω = 1A

Step 3: Calculate voltage drop across each resistor

For R₁ = 4Ω: V₁ = I × R₁ = 1A × 4Ω = 4V

For R₂ = 6Ω: V₂ = I × R₂ = 1A × 6Ω = 6V

Verification: V₁ + V₂ = 4V + 6V = 10V, which equals the battery voltage.

Answer: The total current in the circuit is 1A, with 4V across the 4Ω resistor and 6V across the 6Ω resistor.
