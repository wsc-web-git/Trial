Question 1 (i)

1. (i) In the circuit shown, calculate the equivalent resistance of the circuit and the current flowing in the circuit.

Given: Resistors R₁ = 4Ω and R₂ = 6Ω are connected in series with a battery of 10V.

Think On Your Own

Solution:

Given: R₁ = 4Ω, R₂ = 6Ω, V = 10V

Step 1: Equivalent Resistance

Since the resistors are connected in series, the equivalent resistance R is given by:

R = R₁ + R₂

R = 4Ω + 6Ω

R = 10Ω

Step 2: Current in the Circuit

By Ohm's Law: V = I × R

Therefore, I = V / R

I = 10V / 10Ω

I = 1A

Step 3: Verification

Voltage across 4Ω resistor: V₁ = I × R₁ = 1A × 4Ω = 4V

Voltage across 6Ω resistor: V₂ = I × R₂ = 1A × 6Ω = 6V

Total voltage: V₁ + V₂ = 4V + 6V = 10V (which equals battery voltage)

Answer:

Equivalent resistance of the circuit = 10Ω

Current flowing in the circuit = 1A

You've mastered series resistance and Ohm's Law concepts.

Remember: In series, Rtotal = R₁ + R₂ and current is same through all resistors.
